Admissions

Dream Tree International School operates year round (12 months) with a new school year starting July 1 and ends on June 30. The school admits students on a space available basis and once the classes are full, the student is placed on the waitlist after paying the reservation fee. Waitlisted children will be admitted on a first come first serve basis. Children between the ages of 2 through 6 may enroll in their age specific classes, but we do require children to visit prior to enrollment.

Before children are admitted, parents are required to fill out an enrollment application. Additionally, all students must be up to date on all of their immunizations and a copy of their immunization record must be provided.

All tuition and dues must be paid prior to enrollment in order to secure a slot for your child in our preschool. Further details on tuition and fees can be found in the Tuition and Billing section of this handbook.

Dream Tree International School, as its own discretion, may accept non-DTIS students on a Temporary Status (for a set period less than four months) upon meeting school admission requirements. These students will be integrated into regular programs having minimum impact on our students academically.

Tuition And Billing

Enrollment at Dream Tree International School is on a fiscal year basis which starts on July 1 and ends on June 30 of each year. Subsequently, tuition is to be paid yearly due on June 15 of each year for the upcoming school year. Before a student has been confirmed for enrollment, parents will be required to pay their child's tuition along with the following fees:

• Class Materials

• Non-refundable registration fee

• Application fee

• Deposit

• School uniform and bag

Tuition and any fees are invoiced one month before and due on the 15th and are non-refundable. (i.e. The yearly tuition and fees are invoiced on June 1, and due on June 15 of each school year.) Should the 15th land on a weekend or school holiday, tuition will be due on the following school day. Late payment penalities may be assessed for each day the payment is delinquent. The parents have an option,with an additional feee of 1% of the yearly tuition and fees, to pay on the three term(refer to the "school hours" section) installment plan. For children wishing to be placed on the waitlist, a non-refundable reservation fee is required for confirmation. Once an opening is made available, the reservation fee will applied towards the registration fee. Children enrolling after start of the school year will have their tuition pro-rated. However, all applicable fees and deposit will still be equivalent to a full year's tuition. Student wishing to withdraw permanently must submit school withdrawl form at least two months in advance. Only then the deposit will be refunded. There will not be discount of tuition for being absent for any reason. However, you may request take home material for your child to make-up lessons.

School Hours

The school hours are 8:30 am to 3:10 pm Monday though Friday with instruction beginning at 9:30 am. The school year starts on July 1 and ends on June 30 of each year. The school year is seperated into three terms as follows :

Term 1 - July, August, November, October


Term 2 - November, December, January, February


Term 3 - March, April, May, June


Feel free to stay for a short time after arriving in the morning to help your child settle in. If your child is having a difficult time seperating, please speak with a teacher in order to develop a plan on how to best negotiate departure. If your child will be picked up earlier than the scheduled end of the school day for any reason, then the school must be notified by 9:30 am of that day. If your child will be picked up by someone other than the person listed on the release form signed on the application, we must have a signed written notice to that effect. In an emergency you may call the school, otherwise we will not release your child. After school care is also available for an additional fee for parents not able to pick up their children between 3:10pm - 3:45pm. Please contact the office for program availability, fees and terms. In general, we follow all the official Myanmar public holidays for school closures and one week in August for summer vacation. Additional closures will be enacted for staff development and school cleaning. Please refer to the school calendar given at orientation or on our school website.

Meals & Enrichment Class

Dream Tree International School provided daily prepared meals consisting of a mid-morning snack, lunch, and a late afternoon snack. Our menu varies each day and is thoughtfully planned to give students well rounded nutritious meals. Dream Tree International School may form time to time offer extracurricular enrichment classes, which aim to develop student's interests and overall growth. These classes will be held after normal school hours from 4:00 pm onwards and are voluntary. Parents will be given ample notice to sign up their children and a non-refundable fee for the classs will be due in order to reserve the child's placement in the class.

School Policies

  Child Guidance and Discipline

          At Dream Tree International School we strive to maintain a positive and orderly environment in order to create an atmosphere for high quality learning. We tailor expectations fit the developmental levels of the children to minimize frustration and inappropriate behavior. Our teachers do intervene when a child's behavior becomes disruptive or dangerous to the rest of the students. Teachers will encourage children to develop their own self-control, management of feelings, problem solving, and empathy through praise and positive reinforcement. Good behavior at Dream Tree International School will center on principles of respect, politeness, responsibility and honesty. We do not use any physical punishment, humiliation, or verbal abuse when disciplining children. Also, a child is never punished for wetting, soiling, or failing to use the toilet. Parents will be notified of any ongoing behavioral problems and parent conferences may be scheduled to discuss a plan of action. If the negative behavior continues with no resolution and or the child poses a risk to the safety of other children, suspension or full withdrawal may be considered. Dream Tree International School is fully committed to the safety of the students, staff and visitors. Among safety measures we have, the school will schedule and practice fire drills, CCTV monitoring that are for school use only.


  Drop off and pick up

          All children must have either their parent or guardian sign them in every morning and sign them out when picked up.


  Uniform

          Children are required to attend school in their uniforms and the school P.E. shirt can be worn in place of the uniform. Therefore, an extra set of clean uniform must always be kept at school.


  Bedding and other required items

          Children are required to bring their own kid size beddings to be used in school. Other required item list will be provided separately upon enrollment.


  Toys from home

          Children are not allowed to bring any toys from home. Additionally, school toys are not allowed to take home.


  Healthcare

          Children are required to be current with all of their immunizations. Please observe your child and remember that he or she has the potential for infecting other children, staff and parents. The staff at Dream Tree International School reserves the right to send your child home, if in their judgement, your child is too ill to comfortably be at school or their illness is contagious. You will immediately be notified of our decision and are expected to pick up your child or make arrangements for someone to pick him/her up.If you are unreachable, we will attempt to contact your emergency designee. Your child will be isolated from the others and be kept in a quiet room until your arrival. Every morning we will screen each child for any indications of a fever by taking their temperature. If a child's temperature in the morning is 38 degree C. (100 degree F.)or higher, he/she will not be admitted into the school. Children recovering from illness must not be contagious and have a normal body temperature for at least 34 hours before returning to school. No medication will be administered by the staff.

Medical Procedures

Non-emergency - In the case of a minor injury or accident, staff will administer first aid and if necessary the child's physician will be called. If any treatment is administered, an injury report will be filled out by the teacher and a copy will be provided to the parent. One copy of the report will also be placed in the child's file.


Emergency - In the case of a serious file or life threatening injury, illness, or accident the staff will immediately dial for an ambulance. First aid will be administered, if necessary, and the teacher will accompany the child to the hospital. The teacher will also bring the child's medical authorization consent form and the parents will immediately be contacted. The incident will be documented and placed in the child's file.
